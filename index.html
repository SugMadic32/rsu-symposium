About the Event

Symposium Overview

The RIC ICT Symposium 2026 is an academic event organized by students of Rangsit University International College under the theme "Generative AI and the Future of ICT Learning."

This symposium bridges cutting-edge AI technology and academic education, bringing together students and educators to explore the transformative role of generative AI in modern ICT curricula.

Advance AI Literacy

Build awareness of generative AI tools among ICT students and educators.

Present Original Research

Showcase student-led research on AI coding agents and programming skills.

Foster Collaboration

Create a platform for meaningful exchange between students and faculty.

Shape Future Curricula

Identify how AI tools can be integrated ethically into university coursework.

Expected Outcomes

  • Published research findings on AI coding agents' influence on programming skills
  • A curated educational resource library on generative AI for ICT learners
  • Framework for responsible AI integration in university programming courses
  • Increased awareness of AI tools such as Copilot, ChatGPT, and Claude among RSU students
  • Recommendations for faculty on assessment in an AI-assisted environment
Research Topic

The Influence of AI Coding Agents
on Programming Skills Development

An in-depth study of how AI-powered coding tools reshape the way students acquire, practice, and master programming.

Research Questions

  1. How do AI coding agents affect the rate at which students learn to code?
  2. Do students who use AI tools develop deeper or shallower conceptual understanding?
  3. What role does over-reliance on AI play in skill degradation?
  4. How should educators adapt assessment in the era of AI-assisted coding?

Methodology

Survey-based quantitative research targeting RSU ICT students, supplemented by literature analysis using the Health Belief Model (HBM) and Technology Acceptance Model (TAM).

Introduction

The emergence of AI coding agents marks a pivotal shift in software education. Tools such as GitHub Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini now provide real-time code generation, debugging assistance, and intelligent suggestions. This research examines whether these tools empower learners or inadvertently erode foundational programming skills.

Problem Statement

While AI coding agents demonstrably increase short-term productivity, there is growing concern among educators that students may be bypassing the cognitive struggle necessary for deep skill acquisition. The core tension this research addresses is between AI-assisted efficiency and independent competency development in ICT education.

Benefits of AI Coding Agents

Faster prototyping and iteration
Instant debugging assistance
Code explanation and documentation
Reduces barriers to entry
Available 24/7 without cost
Boilerplate code automation
Accelerates learning new syntax
Ideation and architecture support

Challenges & Limitations

  • Risk of over-reliance reducing independent problem-solving ability
  • Potential for reinforcing incorrect programming habits
  • Security and academic integrity concerns
  • AI-generated code may lack context-specific optimization
  • Ethical questions around intellectual property attribution
  • Unequal access to premium AI tools among students

Future Implications for ICT Education

ICT programs must evolve to embrace AI tools as pedagogical instruments rather than threats. Future curricula should emphasize AI-collaborative problem-solving, critical evaluation of AI outputs, and ethical coding practices. Assessment frameworks need redesigning to test conceptual understanding alongside implementation ability.

Explore Generative AI

What is Generative AI?

Generative AI refers to machine learning models capable of producing new content — text, images, code, audio — based on patterns learned from vast training datasets. Unlike traditional AI focused on classification, generative models create.

Large Language Models

LLMs are the backbone of modern AI coding agents. Trained on billions of tokens including source code repositories, they develop a statistical understanding of programming syntax, patterns, and logic across many languages.

AI in Education

Generative AI is transforming education by enabling personalized learning pathways, instant feedback, content generation, and intelligent tutoring — raising both opportunities and challenges for academic integrity.

What are AI Coding Agents?

AI coding agents are specialized systems designed to assist with software development: writing code, explaining concepts, debugging, documenting, and suggesting improvements in real time within developer environments.

Prompt Engineering

The skill of crafting effective instructions for AI models. Good prompts are specific, contextual, and iterative. In coding contexts, well-structured prompts yield significantly better and more maintainable code outputs.

Transformer Architecture

The neural network architecture powering modern LLMs. Transformers use attention mechanisms to weigh the importance of different input sections, enabling coherent long-form generation including complex code structures.

Code Generation

AI tools can generate working code from natural language descriptions. This accelerates prototyping but requires developers to critically evaluate correctness, security, and alignment with project architecture.

Code Review & Analysis

AI assistants can analyze existing code for bugs, performance issues, security flaws, and style inconsistencies — providing feedback that previously required senior developer time or expensive tools.

Test Generation

Automatically generating unit tests, integration tests, and edge case scenarios is one of AI's highest-value contributions to software quality — covering code paths developers might overlook under pressure.

Documentation

AI can generate docstrings, README files, API documentation, and inline comments from existing code, dramatically reducing the documentation burden in fast-moving projects.

Refactoring Support

AI tools can suggest and execute refactoring operations — renaming variables, extracting methods, optimizing algorithms, and migrating code to newer patterns or frameworks.

Intelligent Debugging

By analyzing stack traces, error messages, and code context, AI debuggers can identify root causes and suggest targeted fixes — often catching issues invisible under time pressure.

Academic Integrity

Using AI tools without disclosure constitutes academic dishonesty at most institutions. Students must understand their university's policies, cite AI assistance, and ensure submitted work reflects genuine understanding.

Avoiding Over-Reliance

Consistently outsourcing problem-solving to AI prevents the struggle necessary for deep learning. Effective use of AI should amplify thinking, not replace it — treat AI as a collaborator, not an answer machine.

Data Privacy

Prompting AI systems with sensitive code or personal data can expose that information. Understand data retention policies and use enterprise or local models when working with confidential projects.

Intellectual Property

The legal status of AI-generated code is still evolving globally. Developers should be aware of training data sources, potential licensing implications, and the importance of understanding all code submitted as their own.

Bias & Fairness

AI models can reflect biases present in their training data — producing code that encodes assumptions or performs differently for certain input categories. Critical review of AI outputs is always essential.

Responsible Use

A responsible AI use policy for students should cover transparency, verification of outputs, documentation of AI contributions, and a commitment to continued independent skill development.

About Rangsit University

Rangsit University (RSU) is a private research university located in Pathum Thani Province, Thailand. Founded in 1986, RSU has grown into one of Thailand's most innovative universities, recognized for its strong emphasis on technology, creativity, and international collaboration.

The International College at RSU offers English-medium programs designed to develop globally competitive graduates. The ICT programs are recognized for their industry relevance and research culture.
